It contains many beneficial minerals such as calcium, manganese, iron, zinc, vitamin B1 and dietary fiber, just to name a few. Sorrel - Otherwise known as roselle or red hibiscus, these flower petals are usually steeped to make a refreshing beverage with little effort and few ingredients. Health highlights include vitamin C, lowering blood pressure, and it is a diuretic. Research suggest it may help contain certain cancers because of it’s flavonoid rich makeup. A rule of thumb is the longer you steep the petals, the stronger the taste and richer the color of your drink!

Aloe vera juice - A skin savior, aloe vera juice is a clear liquid derived from the plant itself. It boasts of skin healing components such as vitamins B, C and E, as well as folic acid and is rich in antioxidants. Try this recipe for the ultimate quick and easy aloe vera detox smoothie.

Tumeric - A bright yellow spice known in South Asian kitchens is making it’s way around the globe. Packed with curcumin, an active compound, it has been shown to be positively beneficial by blocking beta-amyloid plaque which impacts the development of Alzheimer’s disease. With a high antioxidant value and a reputation for curing aches and depression, it should be a staple in your pantry. Brew it into a tea for a nice drink.

    • Taiwan - Papaya Milk This drink is made with 3 easy to find ingredients with an optional 4th: fresh papaya, milk, condensed milk and ice. Just blend the first 3 and pour into a cup, over ice! Try coconut or soy milk for a lactose friendly version.

    Mexico - Agua Fresca Juicy tropical fruits and water are blended and then strained to create the base for this tasty drink. After the mixture is strained, it is poured into a cup and mixed with a dash of sugar or honey. Serve with fruity garnishes.

    India - Mango Lassi Lassi is usually sipped during meals to soothe the palate while eating a spicy meal. It requires fresh mangos, yogurt, milk and sugar. Blend all of the ingredients and pour into a cup. Top with whipped cream if desired.

    Japan - Matcha tea Matcha is a powder version of green tea leaves and can be used to make a variety of healthy and delicious drinks. All you need is 1/4 teaspoon of matcha tea powder and water. Heat the water until it is just before boiling and then whisk in the powder. You can let the mixture cool or drink it hot. Also, you can add sugar and a few teaspoons of fruit juice to make it a fruity tea!
